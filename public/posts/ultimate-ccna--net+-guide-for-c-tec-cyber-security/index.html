Prerequisites#

  1. Create an account & register both volumes of the Official Cert Guide (OCG) Library on Cisco Press as well as Pearson Test Prep
  2. A good attitude. If you believe that you’re going to pass if you put in the effort, it helps.
  3. Install Packet Tracer

Each Week in Class (about 1 book part per week)#

  1. In class: Pay attention, take notes, do labs to the best of your ability, ask questions
  2. Watch all of Jeremy’s IT Lab for the part of the book you’re working on (2x speed makes it easier). Be sure to pause and take notes if needed.
  3. Optionally, do the labs that go along with Jeremy’s CCNA Course (good for topics only briefly mentioned in class)
  4. Speed read through the OCG part, slowing down if there is anything you need more focus on
  5. Do all DIKTA (Do I know the Answer) & Part Review Questions on Pearson Test Prep
  6. Take the quiz assigned in class

Jeremy’s IT Lab CCNA Lectures Mapped to the OCG Parts#

  1. OCG Part 1-1: Introduction to Networking
  2. OCG Part 1-2: Implementing Ethernet LANs
  3. OCG Part 1-3: Implementing VLANs and STP
  4. OCG Part 1-4: IPv4 Addressing (Subnetting)
  5. OCG Part 1-5: IPv4 Routing
  6. OCG Part 1-6: OSPF
  7. OCG Part 1-7: IPv6
  8. OCG Part 2-1: Wireless LANs
  9. OCG Part 2-2: IP Access Control Lists (& TCP/IP)
  10. OCG Part 2-3: Security Services
  11. OCG Part 2-4: IP Services
  12. OCG Part 2-5: Network Architecture
  13. OCG Part 2-6: Network Automation

Before the Network+ Exam#

  1. You should be able to pass Network+ easily if you complete your CCNA studies(including Network Architecture/Automation).
  2. However, I would look at the Exam Objectives and review anything you need to touch up on.
  3. Some of the things I used or wish I used before taking the Net+ are: Port Numbers for CCNA/Net+ Flashcards, Troubleshooting Methodology Flashcards, & Professor Messer’s DNS Records Video. I also wish I finished the last few chapters of the OCG.

Before the CCNA Exam#

  1. Complete all or almost all of the Pearson Test Prep CCNA Exam Content. The per volume exams and the full exams share the same content, so I recommend doing the CCNA Volume 1 Exam 1, Volume 2 Exam 1, CCNA Full Exam 3, and CCNA Full Exam 4. Do the per volume exams using Study mode and the full exam ones using Practice Exam mode. Look at your scores from the CCNA practice exams and focus your study on areas you struggle with.
  2. Look at the CCNA Exam Topics and review anything that you are uncomfortable with. Keywords: Troubleshoot, Configure, Verify, & Interpret are all topics I would do more extensive lab study on. Recognize, Explain, Compare, etc mean that it’s more knowledge based. See pages 594-595 in the OCG Vol. 2 for a chart that goes over this stuff.
  3. Try to Complete the CCNA Final Lab Exam before taking the test. It helps a ton during the lab sections in the real exam. If you get done early, try to complete Jeremy’s IT Lab CCNA MEGA LAB.
  4. Ask the teachers lots of questions to fill as many knowledge gaps as possible before it’s too late.
  5. It helped me to use the earplugs that are provided in the testing center. I think that is one of the reasons I had so much time left when I finished, the earplugs just help you focus. Also, a good meal and sleep helps too.
  6. Read the last chapter of Volume 2. It has more in depth test taking tips, a chart of everything you need to be able to configure, and more helpful stuff.

If you fail a Certification Exam#

  1. Don’t give up hope. These exams are tough. However, failing an exam is one of the best ways to practice the exam.
  2. Immediately after failing, write down in a notebook everything you can remember from the test. You can use your notes about the exam for your own study, but it’s against the NDA to share those notes.
  3. Then look at the exam topics list and think about which areas you could improve on.
